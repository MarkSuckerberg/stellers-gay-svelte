## Birds

The birds are back at last. It's wonderful to be able to go outside and just listen to them all sing once again. The winter birds are all lovely, and truthfully I'll miss the charming little Dark-Eyed Juncos hopping about everywhere, but seeing and hearing my old favourites around is definitely wonderful. The red-winged blackbirds are ever so common, but they're just so handsome. I'd love to draw myself a blackbird fellow of some sort. The robins are lovely to hear, too. I forgot just how much their voices contributed to the song that represents the nature I'm used to. The house finches, specifically the males with their pink-red tinted heads are pretty to see at the feeder. No humminbirds quite yet, but I put out some [homemade nectar](#hummingbird-nectar-recipe). The blue jays haven't gone anywhere, they stay over the winter, but they do seem more active now that there's food to get. Saw a lovely goldfinch the other day, plenty of turkey vultures, a fair few wild turkies, a red-tailed hawk, and then of course a whole HOST of grackles. Some brown-headed cowbirds and european starlings as well, too. Playing some [Flock Around](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3618030/Flock_Around/) with friends has been awful fun as well. There's not a ton to the game, but it's cute and nice to just be silly with.

### Hummingbird Nectar recipe

I usually do 1 cup sugar to 6-8 cups water for most of the year, and then 1 cup of sugar to 4-5 cups water right before migration in the fall. Basically just throw it all in a pot, heat it and stir it until it dissolves, and then bring it to a boil. Then let it cool to room temp of course. Mileage might vary, but this seems to do me well enough.
